# Soul of Love Design Bible

This document defines the unified visual language for the Soul of Love ecosystem:

- Soul of Love Daily Energy
- Matrix 9 Interface
- Soul of Love Oracle
- Portfolio Website
- Future AI-generated artwork
- Marketing, social, album, and spiritual product visuals

It expands from the first three Major Arcana test assets:

- `the-fool.webp`: pearl-white aura, open starlit cliff, gentle beginning
- `the-chariot.webp`: crimson aura, centered momentum, disciplined celestial motion
- `justice.webp`: silver-gold aura, symmetry, calm authority, cosmic scales
- `MASTER_STYLE_REFERENCE_V1`: premium poster architecture, framed oracle card, side energy modules, cinematic gold-on-cosmic hierarchy

Soul of Love is not fear-based fortune telling. It is a premium spiritual self-awareness system. Every image should feel like a mirror, a breath, and a soft invitation to return to the user's inner truth.

## MASTER_STYLE_REFERENCE_V1 Extraction

The master reference establishes the production-level visual language for the Soul of Love universe. It should be treated as the north star for layout hierarchy, premium finish, and visual rhythm.

Core rules extracted from the reference:

- The design is poster-first: a clear editorial hierarchy leads the eye from brand, to title, to central card, to supporting data, to message, to footer.
- The central hero card is the altar. Supporting information orbits it, but never competes with it.
- Premium feeling comes from restraint: dark negative space, thin gold lines, high-contrast ivory type, and carefully placed glow.
- The frame language is refined Art-Deco-oracle: rounded rectangular gold frames, fine corner ornaments, thin dividers, and small star anchors.
- Side modules work like celestial instruments: aura and score are secondary satellites placed around the main card.
- Lighting is hierarchical: strongest light sits inside/behind the card artwork, secondary glow appears around score/aura modules, background stars remain low.
- The camera distance is full-body or medium-full inside the card, while the overall poster camera is straight-on and symmetrical.
- Depth is created by stacked planes: cosmic background, ornament lines, framed card, side modules, message panel, footer.
- Sacred geometry should feel engraved, not drawn loudly. It is fine, metallic, and precise.
- Thai and English typography should feel ceremonial, spacious, and premium when rendered by UI, but generated artwork itself must still contain no text.

## 1. Brand DNA

Soul of Love is a luxury cosmic emotional interface. It blends Thai spiritual sensitivity, symbolic psychology, aura language, and premium digital design.

The brand should feel:

- gentle, not weak
- mystical, not confusing
- sacred, not religiously rigid
- premium, not flashy
- emotional, not dramatic
- intimate, not invasive
- cinematic, not decorative
- symbolic, not deterministic

Before interacting, users should feel curiosity and emotional safety.

During interaction, users should feel seen, softened, and gently guided. The interface should feel like a quiet ritual: a small pause where the user can notice themselves without being judged.

After interacting, users should feel clearer, calmer, and more connected to their own pattern. The product should leave them with language for their inner state, not fear about their future.

Soul of Love is a mirror, not a verdict.

## 2. Soul of Love Signature

The following four elements are the permanent visual language of Soul of Love. They should appear across artworks, UI backgrounds, export cards, oracle visuals, Matrix 9 posters, and future brand imagery.

### Soul Ring

A thin luminous aura ring behind the subject, symbol, or central energy.

Purpose:

- represents the user's inner field
- creates instant Soul of Love recognition
- frames the archetype without making a heavy tarot border
- adds spiritual presence while keeping the image modern

Rules:

- thin, elegant, soft-edged
- usually behind head, heart, object, or central symbol
- never thick, neon, or aggressive
- may be incomplete or partially hidden by glow

### Vertical Light Axis

A soft golden vertical energy line connecting sky, heart, and earth.

Purpose:

- represents alignment
- gives images a premium sacred structure
- anchors cosmic energy into the body and the world
- works especially well for mobile portrait cards

Rules:

- gold or pearl-gold
- subtle, not a hard laser beam
- should pass near the central figure, heart, or symbolic object
- visible enough to be recognized, but not louder than the subject

### Orbit Lines

Two or three elegant orbital energy paths around the subject or symbol.

Purpose:

- represents movement, timing, unseen forces, and energetic relationship
- adds depth and cosmic rhythm
- visually links Daily Energy, Matrix 9, and Oracle systems

Rules:

- use only 2-3 main orbit paths
- thin gold, pearl, or aura-tinted lines
- never turn into busy occult diagrams
- should guide the eye, not trap it

### Sacred Dust

Soft cosmic particles, tiny stars, light fragments, or aura dust.

Purpose:

- gives the brand its breath and atmosphere
- makes images feel alive without clutter
- creates continuity across artwork and UI glow systems

Rules:

- small, soft, and layered
- should float through light, not cover the subject
- avoid glitter overload
- never form readable letters, numbers, or accidental symbols

### Oracle Frame

A refined rounded rectangular frame used when artwork appears inside a card, poster, or export layout.

Purpose:

- creates premium product structure
- separates artwork from information modules
- gives Soul of Love a recognizable editorial card system
- makes hero artwork feel collectible and ceremonial

Rules:

- thin gold or champagne-metallic line
- rounded corners, never sharp heavy frames
- may include tiny corner ornaments, star anchors, and hairline dividers
- should feel engraved and precise, not busy
- must not become a traditional printed tarot border inside generated artwork unless the UI/card template requires it

### Celestial Data Satellites

Small secondary modules that display aura, score, date, mode, or symbolic metadata around the central card.

Purpose:

- turns information into ritual instruments
- supports the central card without competing
- creates premium dashboard/poster hierarchy

Rules:

- use side placement, circular score modules, or small framed capsules
- keep hierarchy clearly below the main card
- use gold hairlines, soft glow, and generous spacing
- never crowd the central hero artwork

## 3. Sacred Geometry Language

Soul of Love does not use random occult geometry. Geometry must be minimal, elegant, and meaningful.

### Circle

Represents wholeness, aura, emotional protection, and the soul field.

Use for:

- Soul Ring
- halos
- completion
- inner safety

### Triangle

Represents direction, choice, transformation, and spiritual focus.

Use for:

- moments of decision
- ascent or descent
- focused intention
- cards like The Magician, The Chariot, Judgement

### Hexagon

Represents harmony, structure, beauty, and energetic architecture.

Use for:

- Matrix 9 expansions
- stability
- sacred order
- cards involving structure, wisdom, or balance

### Orbit

Represents cycles, timing, relationship, and unseen movement.

Use for:

- Wheel of Fortune
- The World
- The Lovers
- energy moving around a person or symbol

### Vertical Axis

Represents alignment between sky, heart, body, and earth.

Use for:

- all hero artworks when possible
- export cards
- portfolio hero sections
- spiritual interface backgrounds

Geometry must support emotion. If the geometry becomes the main character unintentionally, reduce it.

### Hairline Divider

Represents ceremony, pacing, and premium editorial structure.

Use for:

- separating title, date, message, and footer sections
- anchoring small star ornaments
- creating breath between information blocks

Rules:

- extremely thin gold or champagne line
- may end with tiny star points or diamond anchors
- never use thick decorative dividers

### Rounded Oracle Rectangle

Represents containment, ritual focus, and collectible card presence.

Use for:

- central card frame
- quote/message panel
- social export templates

Rules:

- rounded corners
- fine double-line frame allowed
- subtle dark glass fill behind text
- corner ornaments must be delicate and symmetrical

## 4. Aura System

Soul of Love uses aura in layers. These layers must work together without fighting each other.

### Base Artwork Aura

The aura baked into the artwork.

Purpose:

- defines the card's emotional world
- gives each card a core color identity
- should be soft enough to accept app overlays later

Rules:

- avoid fully saturated backgrounds
- keep dark cosmic base visible
- aura should glow around the subject, not flatten the whole image

### Dynamic Runtime Aura Overlay

The app applies aura colors dynamically based on the reading result.

Purpose:

- makes one hero artwork feel different across readings
- connects artwork to Daily Energy score and aura state
- keeps the product alive without needing infinite images

Rules:

- base artwork aura must not overpower runtime overlay
- leave midtone and shadow areas for overlay to tint
- avoid baked-in colors that clash with all other aura colors

### Glow

Glow is emotional emphasis.

Use glow to:

- highlight the Soul Ring
- frame the subject
- reinforce energy score
- soften the transition between artwork and UI

Avoid:

- neon overload
- hard bloom that destroys detail
- glow that makes text unreadable

### Border

Borders are subtle containers, not frames.

Use:

- thin aura-colored or gold edge
- soft inner ring or low-opacity line
- no heavy tarot border

### Particle

Particles are sacred dust.

Use:

- light particles around aura fields
- small gold/pearl cosmic fragments
- gentle motion feeling

Avoid:

- confetti
- glitter storm
- particles over faces or key symbols

### Material Language

Soul of Love materials should feel like a premium spiritual object, not a flat digital graphic.

Use:

- champagne gold hairlines
- pearl-white luminous cores
- smoked glass panels
- soft metallic rims
- deep velvet-cosmic black
- subtle engraved ornament

Avoid:

- flat yellow gold
- plastic neon glow
- heavy bevels
- cheap fantasy metal
- high-saturation UI gradients that overpower the artwork

### Lighting Hierarchy

Light must be ranked.

1. Primary light: inside or behind the central artwork/archetype.
2. Secondary light: aura module, score module, or key data satellite.
3. Tertiary light: sacred dust, background stars, hairline ornaments.

Do not let background stars become brighter than the central card. Do not let side modules overpower the hero artwork.

## 5. Composition System

Not every card should be centered in the same way. Soul of Love uses four composition families.

### Poster Architecture

When creating share cards, product posters, or marketing visuals, use a five-level hierarchy:

1. Brand/title zone
2. Central oracle card altar
3. Celestial data satellites
4. Message panel
5. Footer/ethical note

Rules:

- central card should occupy the visual authority of the page
- side modules must be smaller and quieter than the card
- message panels should use dark glass and thin gold frame language
- footer should feel ceremonial, not like legal clutter
- symmetry is preferred for premium trust; asymmetry is allowed only when movement is the concept

### Focal Hierarchy Standard

For all Soul of Love visuals, the viewer should read:

1. overall energy glow
2. central card/archetype
3. aura/score/date data
4. message
5. brand footer

If the message, particles, or side modules are noticed before the central card, the layout is too busy.

### Human Archetypes

Cards:

- The Fool
- The Magician
- The High Priestess
- The Empress
- The Emperor
- The Hierophant
- The Lovers
- Strength
- The Hermit
- Justice
- Temperance
- The Star
- The Sun
- Judgement
- The World

Camera:

- medium-full to full-body
- slight low angle for dignity, not dominance
- calm frontal or gentle three-quarter pose

Distance:

- subject large enough to read on mobile
- face and gesture visible

Negative space:

- top 12-15% calmer sky/aura
- bottom 18-22% lower-detail space for overlay panel

Focal hierarchy:

1. energy/aura
2. gesture or symbolic object
3. face/character

### Movement Archetypes

Cards:

- The Chariot
- Wheel of Fortune
- The Lovers when choice/movement is emphasized
- Judgement when rising energy is emphasized

Camera:

- forward perspective, controlled diagonal, or centered motion axis
- should feel like movement with discipline

Distance:

- subject/symbol should remain readable as a silhouette

Negative space:

- leave front direction clear
- do not fill the image with motion trails

Focal hierarchy:

1. direction
2. momentum
3. subject or vehicle

### Cosmic Archetypes

Cards:

- Wheel of Fortune
- The Moon
- The Sun
- The Star
- The World
- The High Priestess when symbol-led

Camera:

- wider cosmic scale
- symbolic object may be larger than the figure
- central circle/orbit structure is allowed

Distance:

- use depth and atmosphere
- subject can be smaller if the energy is instantly clear

Negative space:

- preserve large dark cosmic fields for UI contrast

Focal hierarchy:

1. cosmic phenomenon
2. symbolic geometry
3. figure, if present

### Transformation Archetypes

Cards:

- Death
- The Tower
- The Devil
- The Hanged Man
- Judgement

Camera:

- threshold, mirror, vertical release, or suspended perspective
- avoid horror staging

Distance:

- enough space for transformation to feel symbolic, not literal violence

Negative space:

- use dark-to-light transition areas
- leave room for aura glow and UI overlay

Focal hierarchy:

1. transformation energy
2. threshold or release symbol
3. character/object

## 6. Character Language

### Age Range

Characters should generally feel adult, timeless, and emotionally mature. Avoid childish styling unless The Sun needs childlike warmth, and even then keep it symbolic rather than literal.

### Realism Level

High-detail digital painting with cinematic realism, softened by spiritual stylization. Avoid cartoon, anime, game splash art, and hyper-real portrait photography.

### Facial Diversity

Faces should vary across the full set. Avoid repeating one idealized face. Use diverse facial structures, skin tones, and expressions while keeping the same premium Soul of Love atmosphere.

### Body Language

Body language should communicate energy before personality.

Examples:

- The Fool: open chest, lifted face, light step
- Justice: still shoulders, balanced arms, composed presence
- The Chariot: forward alignment, controlled posture
- The Hermit: inward turn, lantern close to body

### Costume Language

Costumes should feel luxury spiritual, not fantasy battle gear.

Use:

- flowing fabrics
- subtle metallic details
- translucent layers
- celestial ornaments
- refined gold accents
- Thai-inspired elegance only when subtle and respectful

Avoid:

- fantasy armor overload
- RPG character design
- horror robes
- aggressive spikes
- sexualized costumes
- literal religious costumes that imply one fixed doctrine

## 7. Energy Before Character

Soul of Love philosophy:

The user should perceive the energy first, then the archetype, then the character.

The artwork is not a portrait. It is a field of meaning.

Examples:

- The Star: hope before woman.
- Justice: balance before scales.
- The Fool: freedom before traveler.
- The Chariot: direction before vehicle.
- Death: transformation before ending.
- The Sun: vitality before sun disc.
- The Moon: uncertainty before moon symbol.

This principle protects the brand from becoming costume illustration. The art must express emotional energy before depicting a tarot scene.

## 8. Mobile First Safe Zones

Soul of Love artwork must work inside small mobile cards, export posters, and responsive UI panels.

### Top Safe Zone

- top 12-15% should be calmer and lower-detail
- good for labels, badges, or app chrome
- avoid faces, hands, or key symbols here

### Bottom Overlay Zone

- bottom 18-22% should support a dark translucent HTML/CSS panel
- avoid important faces, hands, sacred objects, and tiny detail
- lower contrast is preferred behind text

### Center Focal Zone

- primary energy should live between 28-62% vertical height
- figure face, symbolic object, or light source should be readable at mobile size

### Minimum Contrast

- title panels need dark enough backing
- avoid bright white/gold directly behind expected text zones
- keep high-detail geometry away from text zones

### Readable Export Card Rules

- no image text
- no complex detail behind exported title/message areas
- aura glow should guide attention, not reduce readability
- subject must remain recognizable in a 360px-wide mobile preview
- use large contrast steps: ivory/gold text on smoked glass, not text directly on busy nebula
- central card should have its own contained frame so artwork detail does not fight the surrounding poster
- score/aura modules should be readable at thumbnail size without stealing focus
- message panel should have a quiet dark interior, thin gold edge, and generous line height
- footer must remain legible but visually quieter than the message

## 11. Future Expansion

### Daily Energy

Major Arcana hero art becomes the emotional center of each daily reading. Runtime aura overlays create variation without requiring new artwork for every reading state.

### Matrix 9

The Soul Ring, Vertical Light Axis, Orbit Lines, and Sacred Dust can frame Matrix 9 posters, number grids, and Being Architecture visuals. Matrix artwork should feel like energy architecture rather than tarot.

### Oracle

Oracle cards can use the same visual grammar with broader symbolic subjects. Each oracle concept should still follow energy-before-character and mobile safe-zone rules.

### Portfolio

The portfolio website should use the same dark cosmic base, soft aura gradients, gold geometry, and premium emotional restraint. It should feel like entering the Soul of Love studio universe.

### Marketing Artwork

Marketing visuals should use fewer elements, not more. A single Soul Ring, one aura glow, and one short emotional message should be enough.

### Album Artwork

Album visuals can be more atmospheric and cinematic, but must preserve the four signatures and avoid horror/fear-based spirituality.

## Soul of Love Design Principles

These rules are immutable for future artwork.

1. Energy comes before character.
2. Every artwork must include the Soul Ring, Vertical Light Axis, Orbit Lines, and Sacred Dust unless intentionally cropped for a specific product format.
3. No generated image may contain text, letters, typography, numbers, logos, or watermarks.
4. The mood must be mystical and emotionally safe, never scary or fear-based.
5. Geometry must be minimal, meaningful, and elegant.
6. Aura color must support the reading, not overpower the composition.
7. Every image must work on mobile first.
8. Bottom overlay space must remain readable for HTML/CSS text.
9. The style must feel premium spiritual, not fantasy RPG, horror, or traditional tarot reproduction.
10. Soul of Love is a mirror, not a verdict.
11. MASTER_STYLE_REFERENCE_V1 is the benchmark for premium poster hierarchy, frame language, and visual rhythm.
12. The central oracle card is always the altar; supporting data must orbit it quietly.
13. Use champagne-gold hairlines, pearl highlights, smoked glass, and deep cosmic negative space as the core material language.
14. Sacred geometry must look engraved and intentional, never like random occult decoration.
15. Typography belongs to the UI/poster layer, not inside generated hero artwork.
